HOW I WORK

Four stages, played in order — every time.

01 · Listen

Sit with the people who use the thing daily before touching a single screen. Interviews, shadowing, support-ticket archaeology.

02 · Frame

Turn what I heard into a problem worth solving — not a feature list. Flows, IA, and the constraints that actually matter get written down.

03 · Build

Prototype in fidelity that matches the risk. Cheap and rough for direction, pixel-true for the moments that decide trust.

04 · Prove

Test with real users, instrument what shipped, and come back to the numbers. Design isn't done at handoff.

ABOUT

I studied engineering before design — it still shows.

I started in mechanical engineering, which is probably why I still think of interfaces as systems with tolerances: every screen has a load it needs to carry, and a failure mode when it's pushed too far. I moved into UX in 2019 and have spent the years since working mostly on dense, high-stakes software — logistics, fintech, clinical tools — where the job isn't decoration, it's making complexity survivable.

Outside of client work I mentor early-career designers through a local design collective, and I keep a small archive of bad interfaces I photograph in the wild — ATMs, parking meters, hospital kiosks — as a reminder of what happens when no one owned the experience.
